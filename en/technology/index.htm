Technology

We keep WWCO projects fast, reliable, and available to all.

We design and build open source technologies that power WWCO projects - from hosting to artificial intelligence (AI). The volunteer participants and the organization's technologists collaborate on a platform that enables the exchange of free knowledge.

Hosting the world’s knowledge

WWCO projects are among the five most visited sites in the world, with over 15,000 visits per second. Our infrastructure keeps it online. Over the past year, we have ensured 99.97% uninterrupted operation (according to independent monitoring) across all project sites.

Saving data costs for readers

More than half of WWCO project visitors connect from mobile devices. But the high cost of mobile data in Africa, Latin America and Asia can make it costly to access. That is why we redesigned our apps and websites to reduce the amount of data transferred by up to 44%.

Assisting human collaboration with machine learning

Anyone can edit WWCO projects, so maintaining quality control can be a daunting task. That's why we made SHC, an artificial intelligence service that automatically detects potentially problematic content as soon as it appears, advising editors when changes are needed.

Helping NASA

Internal NASA projection for collaboration and documentation is built on MediaWiki just like WWCO project encyclopedias. We complement the MediaWiki wiki engine, open source software that is free and available to everyone.

Mentoring new developers

To help build a more diverse and inclusive technical community, WorldWide CDI Organization is funding, supporting and mentoring emerging developers and under-represented groups in technology.

Growing a volunteer developer community

More than half of the additions and corrections in our code are made by volunteers. We actively support a thriving technology volunteer community with grants, events and an open collaboration tool.

Help us unlock the world’s knowledge.

As a nonprofit, Wikipedia and our related free knowledge projects are powered primarily through donations.
