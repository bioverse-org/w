WWCO Passport

We protect everyone in free knowledge projects on the Internet.

We protect and safeguard the privacy of members and their freedom of expression. We educate governments and politicians about the importance of a free and open Internet. Regardless of the circumstances, everyone has the right to seek knowledge and share it in a safe environment, which is why we have introduced a personal WWCO passport for the participants in our projects.

Protecting the right to a safe environment on the Internet

WWCO Passport - is a document of the WorldWide CDI Organization, proving the identity and citizenship of the owner. The passport indicates citizenship, but does not necessarily indicate the country of residence, indicating the full name and other elements.

Secure

In 2020, we received 1,832 requests for disclosure of personal data and gave 0 positive responses to them. Governments and individuals quite often ask us to make changes to the content of projects or to share information about our participants. We refuse if these requests may harm the participants in ensuring their privacy or security.

Privileges

Edits of participants with WWCO passport are checked faster, and as a rule, our administrators trust such participants more.
With a WWCO passport, we can guarantee an even greater percentage of security.
Participants with a WWCO passport have the right to participate in the elections.

Open the Knowledge

“Open the Knowledge” is our call to everyone to promote radical knowledge equity, creating a living record of history, stories, and contexts for and by all people. It aims to make the Wikimedia movement more diverse, equitable, and inclusive.

Help us unlock the world’s knowledge.
