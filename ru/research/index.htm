Research

We find ways to make WWCO Projects better.

Our goal — is to make the products of our organization available to everyone, to improve their qualities for people seeking knowledge around the world. Research helps us meet our mission. We turn research questions into ways to empower new readers and contributors. We use research results to inform about new technologies, products and programs. We partner with researchers around the world to better understand the process of creating and sharing knowledge.

Building a positive community culture

Bullying and harassment is a common problem on the Internet, and our products are no exception. We are working to understand and combat harassment in WWCO projects and discussion spaces.

Simplification of citing and referencing sources

In open knowledge projects such as Wikipedia or WWCO Projects, verifiable source links play a decisive role. We are building an open source repository to simplify citation management.

Understanding reader behavior

8,500 people visit our knowledge projects every second. We would like to know why. Our research aims to understand the motivation and needs of readers so that we can identify opportunities for improvement.

Increasing editor diversity

WWCO Projects — it is a global movement, but it was easier for some users to participate than others. We partner with communities to understand why.

Helping new editors

Learning to contribute to WWCO projects can be challenging. We worked with new editors in South Korea and the Czech Republic to understand why they are editing and how we can make it easier for them.

Protecting the open internet

The law should encourage Internet users to stay on the sidelines of editorial decisions so that people can freely share and speak up. We explore how politics affects the way information is exchanged online.
